Telling Stories About the Past: Rules & Examples

Rule / Explanation

To tell a story about a past event or trip in Spanish, use the Past Simple tense (Pretérito Indefinido) for completed actions. Structure your story with linking words to show the order of events: primero (first), luego (then), después de eso (after that), de repente (suddenly), and al final (in the end). These words help your listener follow the beginning, the problem, and the ending of your story.

Key Points

  • Past Simple endings — Regular -ar verbs add -é, -aste, -ó, -amos, -asteis, -aron; regular -er/-ir verbs add -í, -iste, -ió, -imos, -isteis, -ieron. Use them for completed past actions like viajé (I traveled) or decidí (I decided).
  • Storytelling linking words — Words like primero (first), luego (then), después de eso (after that), de repente (suddenly), and al final (in the end) organize your narrative and show time order.
  • Phrasal verbs for past experiences — Use salir (go out), volver (come back), and descubrir (find out) to describe actions during a trip or event. For example: salí con mis amigos (I went out with my friends).
  • Vocabulary for experiences — Key nouns include la experiencia (experience), el recuerdo (memory), el evento (event), el viaje (trip), and las vacaciones (holiday). Adjectives like emocionante (exciting), aburrido (boring), and sorprendido (surprised) describe how you felt.

Examples

  • ✓ Primero, viajé a la playa con mi familia. — "First, I traveled to the beach with my family." Uses primero to start the story and Past Simple viajé.
  • ✓ Luego, salimos a caminar por la ciudad. — "Then, we went out to walk around the city." Uses luego and the phrasal verb salir in Past Simple.
  • ✓ Después de eso, de repente empezó a llover. — "After that, suddenly it started to rain." Links events with después de eso and adds de repente for surprise.

Expressing Opinions & Arguments in Spanish

Rule / Explanation

To express and justify opinions in Spanish, use linking words like porque (because), sin embargo (however), and por ejemplo (for example). Modal verbs such as deber (should), tener que (have to), and no tener que (don't have to) help you give reasons or obligations. Phrasal verbs like pensar en (think about), hablar de (talk about), and idear (come up with) are useful for discussing ideas.

Key Points

  • Porque — Means 'because'; it introduces a reason for your opinion. Example: Es importante porque...
  • Sin embargo — Means 'however'; it contrasts two ideas. Example: Es difícil, sin embargo, es útil.
  • Por ejemplo — Means 'for example'; it gives a specific instance to support your argument.
  • Deber — Means 'should'; it expresses advice or recommendation. Example: Debemos aprender inglés.
  • Tener que / No tener que — 'Have to' / 'Don't have to'; shows obligation or lack of it.
  • Pensar en / Hablar de / Idear — Phrasal verbs for 'think about', 'talk about', and 'come up with'.

Examples

  • ✓ El inglés es importante para el futuro porque abre muchas puertas. — Uses porque to justify the opinion.
  • ✓ Es difícil aprender inglés, sin embargo, es muy útil. — Uses sin embargo to contrast difficulty with usefulness.
  • ✓ Por ejemplo, muchos trabajos piden inglés. — Uses por ejemplo to give a concrete example.
  • ✓ Debemos practicar inglés todos los días. — Uses deber to express a recommendation.
  • ✓ Tengo que estudiar vocabulario nuevo. — Uses tener que to show obligation.
  • ✓ No tienes que ser perfecto, solo hablar con confianza. — Uses no tener que to indicate lack of obligation.
